| Channel | Width | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Kaiwi (Molokai) | 26 nm | Oahu-Molokai |
| Pailolo | 8 nm | Molokai-Maui |
| Auau | 9 nm | Maui-Lanai |

**Alenuihaha Channel Warning:**
- Known as most dangerous Hawaiian channel
- Strong winds, turbulence
- Wind acceleration between islands
- Plan for 25+ knot headwinds

## Weather Patterns

### Trade Winds
- Northeast 15-25 knots typical
- Bring moisture to windward sides
- Best flying in morning
- Stronger in afternoon

### Windward vs Leeward
| Side | Weather | Flying |
|------|---------|--------|
| Windward (NE) | Wet, cloudy, showers | Bumpy, reduced vis |
| Leeward (SW) | Dry, sunny, clear | Smooth, good vis |

### Orographic Effects
- Clouds build on mountain slopes
- Rain shadows on lee side
- Venturi effects in valleys
- Strong updrafts/downdrafts

### Seasonal Patterns

**Summer (May-Oct):**
- Consistent trades
- Less rain
- Best flying season

**Winter (Nov-Apr):**
- Kona winds (south/west)
- More variable
- Occasional storms
- Whale season!

## Scenic Flight Routes

### Circle Oahu
```
PHNL → Diamond Head → Waikiki → Pearl Harbor →
North Shore → Dole Plantation → PHNL
Distance: ~80 nm
Aircraft: Any GA
Highlights: Iconic landmarks, beaches, surf spots
Airspace: Mind the Class B!
```

### Maui Volcano & Valley
```
PHOG → Haleakala Crater → Hana Coast →
Iao Valley → West Maui → PHOG
Distance: ~100 nm
Aircraft: Any GA
Highlights: 10,023 ft crater, waterfalls, cliffs
Note: Stay above 3,000 ft AGL over crater
```

### Big Island Volcanoes
```
PHKO → Mauna Loa → Kilauea (volcano) →
Chain of Craters → PHTO → PHKO
Distance: ~150 nm
Aircraft: Any GA
Highlights: Active lava (when flowing), craters
Warning: Volcanic fumes, check NOTAMS
```

### Na Pali Coast (Kauai)
```
PHLI → Waimea Canyon → Na Pali Coast →
Princeville → PHLI
Distance: ~70 nm
Aircraft: Any GA (helicopter ideal)
Highlights: Most dramatic scenery in Hawaii
Best time: Morning for light, calm
```

### Four Island Hop
```
PHNL → PHMK (Molokai) → PHOG (Maui) →
PHNY (Lanai) → PHNL
Distance: ~200 nm
Aircraft: Any GA
Highlights: Island diversity, channels
Time: Half day
```

## Airspace & Procedures

### Honolulu Class B
- Surface to 9,000 ft

## Challenges & Tips

### Ocean Crossings
- Over-water flight planning
- Life vests recommended (RL)
- Know glide distance
- Monitor weather changes

### Volcanic Activity
- Check Kilauea status
- Sulfur dioxide plumes
- Visibility reduction (vog)
- NOTAMS for restrictions

### Mountain Weather
- Clouds build rapidly
- Afternoon thermals
- Wind shear near peaks
- Density altitude at elevation

### Tourism
- Heavy helicopter traffic
- Sight-seeing congestion
- Right of way awareness
- Radio congestion

## Best Practices

### Flight Planning
- Morning flights = best weather
- Fuel at each island (limited options)
- Alternate always planned
- Channel weather can change fast

### Photography Tips
- Fly morning for best light
- Doors off if permitted (helo)
- Windward side = greener
- Volcanos best in golden hour

### Environmental
- Stay 500 ft from wildlife
- Whale overflight rules (winter)
- Minimize noise over residences
- Follow voluntary quiet routes
