## Why Fly Florida?

- **Flat terrain** - Easy navigation, few obstacles
- **Year-round flying** - Great weather most days
- **Diverse scenery** - Beaches, swamps, cities, islands
- **Major airports** - International hub experience
- **GA paradise** - Countless small airports
- **Unique challenges** - Thunderstorms, TFRs, busy airspace

## Weather

### Typical Conditions by Season

| Season | Weather | Flying Quality |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| **Winter** (Dec-Feb) | Cool, dry, clear | Excellent |
| **Spring** (Mar-May) | Warming, occasional storms | Very Good |
| **Summer** (Jun-Sep) | Hot, daily thunderstorms | Mornings Only |
| **Fall** (Oct-Nov) | Cooling, hurricane season end | Good |

### The Afternoon Thunderstorm Pattern

**Summer Reality:**
```
Morning: Clear, calm
10 AM: Cumulus building
1 PM: Towering cumulus
3 PM: Thunderstorms develop
4-6 PM: Peak storm activity
7 PM: Storms dissipate
Evening: Clear again
```

**Strategy:** Fly early! Be on the ground by 2 PM in summer.

### Hurricane Season
- **Official:** June 1 - November 30
- **Peak:** August - October
- Check forecasts before any flights
- Know evacuation procedures
- TFRs during storms

### Sea Breeze Effects
- Afternoon sea breezes develop daily
- Can shift winds 180 degrees
- Creates convergence zones inland
- Triggers afternoon thunderstorms

## Scenic Routes

### The Keys Run (Classic)
```
Route: KMIA → KMTH → KEYW
Distance: 130 nm
Duration: 1.5 hrs (C172)
Altitude: 1,000-2,000 ft
Highlights:
- Biscayne Bay departure
- Card Sound
- Key Largo
- Seven Mile Bridge
- Key West approach over water
Best: Morning, clear day
```

### Everglades Explorer
```
Route: KTMB → X01 (Everglades) → KAPF
Distance: 100 nm
Duration: 1 hr
Altitude: 500-1,000 ft (low is best)
```

### Keys Flying Tips
1. **Always have altitude for glide to land**
2. **Watch for crosswinds** - Exposed runways

4. **Weather** - Can change quickly

## Weather Strategies

### Summer Flying
```
Wake up: 5 AM
Preflight: 6 AM
Takeoff: 7 AM
Land: 11 AM - 1 PM LATEST
Avoid: 2 PM - 7 PM
```

### Thunderstorm Avoidance
1. **Morning flights only** in summer
2. **Check radar** before and during
3. **Have alternate airports** planned
4. **Don't push it** - Land early
5. **Wait it out** - Storms pass quickly

### Winter Fronts
- Cold fronts bring strong winds
- Crosswind training opportunity
- Clear air after front passes
- Plan for wind gusts to 30+ kt

## Checklist: Florida Flight Planning

### Before You Go
- [ ] Weather briefing (thunderstorms?)
- [ ] TFR check (Disney, launches, VIP)
- [ ] MOA activity check
- [ ] Fuel planning
- [ ] Alternate airports identified
- [ ] Water survival gear (overwater)

### In Flight
- [ ] Monitor weather radar
- [ ] Update weather frequently
- [ ] Know nearest airport always
- [ ] Watch for cumulus buildup
- [ ] Stay below/around Class B shelves

### Summer Specific
- [ ] Early morning departure
- [ ] Ground by early afternoon
- [ ] Bring water (hot cockpits!)
- [ ] Sunglasses essential
- [ ] Plan for heat delays
