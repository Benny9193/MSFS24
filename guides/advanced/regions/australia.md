## Weather Patterns

### Climate Zones

| Zone | Area | Weather |
|------|------|---------|
| Tropical | Northern QLD, Top End | Wet/dry seasons |
| Arid | Central/Western Australia | Hot, dry, clear |
| Temperate | Southern coast | Four seasons |
| Mediterranean | Perth area | Dry summer, wet winter |

### Seasonal Considerations

**Wet Season (Nov-Apr, Tropical):**
- Intense thunderstorms
- Flooding
- Cyclones possible
- Not recommended for flying

**Dry Season (May-Oct, Tropical):**
- Best flying weather
- Clear skies
- Accessible outback strips

**Southern Seasons:**
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Hot, bushfire risk
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Cooler, some fog
- Spring/Autumn: Best flying

### Hazards
| Hazard | Where | When |
|--------|-------|------|
| Thunderstorms | Northern | Wet season |
| Dust storms | Outback | Summer |
| Bushfire smoke | Southeast | Summer |
| Sea breeze | Coastal | Afternoon |
| Fog | Victoria, Tasmania | Winter mornings |

## Long-Distance Planning

### Fuel Planning
- Many strips >500 nm apart
- Avgas availability limited outback
- Plan fuel stops carefully
- Drums at some stations (arrange ahead)

### Distances
| Route | Distance | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Sydney → Perth | 2,045 nm | Australia's width |
| Darwin → Adelaide | 1,510 nm | North to south |
| Cairns → Sydney | 1,175 nm | East coast |
| Perth → Darwin | 1,620 nm | Western route |

### Sector Planning Example
Sydney to Perth:
```
YSSY → YBHI (Broken Hill, 500 nm) →
YPED (Adelaide, 255 nm) →
YPKA (Kalgoorlie, 560 nm) →
YPPH (Perth, 330 nm)
Total: 1,645 nm
Fuel stops: 3-4
Time: 12+ hours
```

## Airspace

### Australian Structure
| Class | Type | Service |
|-------|------|---------|
| A | High level | Controlled |
| C | Terminal | Controlled |
| D | Tower | Controlled |
| E | Controlled | Limited |
| G | Uncontrolled | CTAF |

### CTAF (Common Traffic Advisory Frequency)
- Uncontrolled aerodromes
- All aircraft self-announce
- "ALL STATIONS [airport name]"
- Standard position reports

### Major TMA
- Sydney: Complex, multiple airports
- Melbourne: Avalon nearby
- Brisbane: Sunshine Coast nearby

### Outback Communications
- Limited radar coverage
- HF radio recommended
- Position reports critical
- Flight following limited

## Unique Australian Challenges

### Heat
- Density altitude at desert strips
- Surface temps 45°C+ (113°F)
- Fly early morning
- Performance calculations essential

### Wildlife
- Kangaroos on runways (common!)
- Emus, wombats
- Low pass to clear
- More common dawn/dusk

### Distances
- Australia is HUGE
- Don't underestimate sectors
- Fatigue management
- Plan overnight stops

### Remote Area Flying
- No services for 100s of miles
- Carry survival gear
- Water essential
- Emergency beacon (ELT/PLB)

## Quick Reference

### ICAO Prefix
- **Y** = Australia

### Units
| Measurement | Unit |
|-------------|------|
| Altimeter | hPa (QNH) |
| Visibility | meters |
| Wind | knots |
| Runway | meters |
| Temperature | Celsius |

### Transition Altitude
- 10,000 ft (standard)
- Area QNH below
- Standard (1013 hPa) above

### Rules of the Air
- Give way to right
- CTAF procedures
- Flight notification for remote areas
- SARTIME (Search and Rescue Time)

### Emergency Kit (Outback)
- Water (lots!)
- Food
- First aid
- Emergency beacon
- Signaling devices
- Shelter/shade
- Sun protection

### Checklist
- [ ] Fuel planned + reserves
